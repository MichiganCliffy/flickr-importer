Oct 18th - Oct 20th: In route somewhere over the pacific.

Oct 20th - Oct 21st: Getting wined and dined at Taltarni Vineyards. I stayed (courtesy of Taltarni) at the Warrenmang resort while visiting the Pyrenees wine region in Victoria,a couple of hours from Melbourne. While there, I got to watch the Taltarni Cup, a country horse (and camel) race. This is in addition to tasting some fantastic wines and eating some fantastic food.

Oct 22nd: From Taltarni, a painful (hungover) drive to the coast via the Grampians National Park. Along the way I saw a pair of Red Kangaroo's. I tried to stop and get pictures, but they were moving way too fast (they kept up with my car as I drove along the road). In the Grampians, I hiked up to Boronia Peak, which offered some great views across the flatlands to the east and the hills to the west. After a nap, and a few missed turns, I reached Port Campbell on the coast.

Oct 22nd - Oct 23rd: A leasurly Drive along the Great Ocean Road back to Melbourne. I visited two sections of the Great Ocean Road: The Shipwreck Coast and The Surf Coast.

The Shipwreck Coast is spectacular, with all sorts of cool cliffs and rock formations. Some of ths sights I saw along the way were:

  • The Bay of Martyrs
  • The Grotto
  • Loch Ard Gorge
  • and The Twelve Apostles

The Surf Coast is all about sandy beaches and cool little surf towns.

Oct 25th - Nov 1st: Trekking around Tasmania with Anne and Jeff. We started by driving up the east coast to the Freycinet National Park. We went on a hike to the beautiful Wineglass Bay and spent the night at a campsite with some friends of Anne and Jeff. As we were packing up the next morning, we had a visit from a Wallaby with a little baby Joey. From there, we drove up to the Tamar Valley region, stopping along the way to take in the sites, including a secluded sand dune beach. We then went wine tasting at a couple of vineyards around the Tamar River and then headed off to the Cradle Mountain National Park.

Cradle Mountain was definitely one of the highlights of the trip. The first evening there, we went on a couple of quick, short hikes to check things out (and hopefully see a Tasmanian Devil). We didn't see any Tasmanian Devil's, but we did see a whole bunch of Wallaby's and a few Wombats. After a few drinks at the lodge (and a few more back at the ol' cozy cabin) we turned in. The next day we hiked around Lake Dove. It was one of the best days we had in Tasmania, we actually saw the sun! The hike provided a great view of the peak, plus some real interesting snow covered, wooded sections - a few hours well spent.

From the Cradle Mountains, we headed for Strahan, where we would go on a boat tour around the Macquarie Harbour. When we got to Strahan, we decided to see if we could hike out to see Hell's Gate. Somehow we found ourselves on the beach, in our car, crusin on the beach, much to the amusement of a coupla locals. The boat ride was a little long, but it did have some pretty interesting highlights:

  • visit past Hell's Gate
  • stopped of at Sarah's Island, an old penal colony
  • cruise down the Gordon River
  • quick hike through the rainforrest
  • and even saw a platapus (well, we think it was one, it was pretty far off).

At this point, Anne had to leave us to go back to work (boo). The rest of us went on a quick hike up to Hogarth Falls, the a hike to see Nelson Falls, finally stopping at the southern part of Cradle Mountain, around Lake St. Clair. The next day we went on a hike on the shore of the lake, then slowly made our way back to Hobart.

Nov 1st - Nov 6th: Sadly had to say goodbye to Anne and Jeff, and headed to Canberra to meet up with my Aunt Mary. We spent the night at my Cousin Mark's place, giving me the opportunity to be a jungle gym for his three young kids. Mark was not there, unfortunately, but I met his wife Joann and their rambunctious kids. From there we drove down to Cobargo, south along the coast, to Mary and Dave's place.

I thoroughly enjoyed my time with Mary and Dave. We explored the coast, ate some cheese at Tilba, went whale watching and saw some seal lions, checked out a vineyard, and had the odd glass of wine (or three).

Nov 6th - Nov 8th: I bid Mary and Dave goodbye, and slowly made my way up the coast (part of which is called the Grand Pacific Drive).

Nov 8th: Travel back in time to San Francisco (arrived before I left). Relived this day twice.
